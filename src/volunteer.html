Ministries


Music

Liturgical music is an integral part of our worship celebrations. Voices, cantors, and musicians of all ages are welcome. Anyone interested in sharing their music gifts is encouraged to offer their talents to the parish. Contact: Mary Ann Llamedo

Extraordinary Ministers of Holy Communion

Parishioners assist with the distribution of the Eucharist (Body and Blood) at weekday, weekend and Holy Days of Obligations Masses. Extraordinary MInisters of Holy Communion (also known as Eucharistic Ministers) must be 18 years of age, have received the Sacrament of Confirmation in the Catholic Church, and, if married, must be recognized by the Church. Training is on-going. Contact: Cleo Bala-Casino

Lector

Lectors proclaim the Word of the Lord during the LIturgy and, when there is no Deacon present, the General Intercessions. A lector must have a passion for Scripture; an ability to interpret and understand Scripture; and, sufficient public speaking skills to proclaim the Word to the assembly. Training in the specifics of the ministry is provided throughout the year. Parishioners are trained to proclaim the Word of God at all Masses. Lectors are provided with Lector Handbook to assist in their proclamation and pronunciation. Contact: Gloria Rodrigues

Altar Server

Altar Servers offer service to God and the community by assisting the priest during Mass. The ministry is open to all youth of the parish who have made their First Communion and whose parent believe they are ready to serve and responsible enough to be trained. Young men and women assist the Priest and Deacon at the altar for all weekend, Holy Days of Obligation, and special occasion masses. Adults assist during the weekday masses or at special masses when no Altar Servers are available. Contact: Angie Balbuena

Hospitality

Hospitality ministers the first person one meets when they arrive at the church for any celebration. Their warm, friendly smile and welcoming handshake set the tone for the worship and experience. A pleasant, outgoing personality is important for greeters, who may also assist with ushering at any given Sunday Mass or special occasion. Ushers assist at each Saturday vigil and Sunday Masses, Holy Days of Obligation, and other special occasion Masses as needed. Responsibilities include direction crowd flow and movement, offertory of the gifts, assist those with special needs and direct traffic in the parking lot. Contact: Karen Correia-Lemana

Liturgical Environment

The Liturgical Environment Ministry is responsible for the church’s visual enhancement throughout the different liturgical seasons and annual celebrations through Altar and other ornaments, flowers, plants, etc.. The ministry’s goal is to create an inviting and fitting environment of worship, a place that is suited for sacred celebrations, that is dignified and beautiful. The ministry strives to provide an ambiance of tranquility and beauty that prepares the human heart to receive God’s word so the parishioners can enter more fully into communion with God and focus on the liturgy and prayer. Contact: Roselani Ramones

Councils


Finance

The Finance Council is parallel to the Pastoral Council and assists the Pastor with the management of parish funds. Using the Parish Pastoral Plan as a guide, they do long-range financial planning to help insure that funds needed to achieve the goals are available. They work with the parish ministry leaders to help them create budgets that reflect the goals for their particular ministries. If funds need to be raised, they oversee any development efforts in the parish. They monitor the spending of the funds and provide the Pastoral Council with information needed for their assessment and evaluation responsibilities. Thus, the Finance Council Representative attends Pastoral Council meetings in order to keep the line of communication open between the Finance Council and the Pastoral Council, to be a link to the Finance Council about deliberations that have financial implications with the ultimate goal that the Finance Council and the Pastoral Council work in synchrony.

Pastoral

The parish Pastoral Council (Council) is an advisory group to the Pastor regarding the spiritual life of the parish on issues of Liturgy & Prayer, Faith Formation, Outreach & Care, and Stewardship in relation to the overall mission/vision of the parish.

Stewardship

The Finance Council (FC) is parallel to the Council and assists the Pastor with the management of parish funds. Using the Parish Pastoral Plan as a guide, they do long-range financial planning to help insure that funds needed to achieve the goals are available. They work with the parish ministry leaders to help them create budgets that reflect the goals for their particular ministries. Pastoral Council Committees


Parish Planning and Building Committee

Responsible for the maintenance of the facilities and to provide a safe, clean, comfortable, and inviting environment.

Education Committee

Responsible in planning the adult education and formation of parishioners including seminars for Advent and Lent.

Liturgy Committee

Discerns the parish’s worship, liturgical and prayer needs; coordinate the planning and scheduling of the liturgical celebrations for seasonal and special celebrations. Coordinate, support, and evaluate all liturgical ministries in the parish, i.e. lectors, extraordinary ministers of the holy communion, altar servers, cantors, musicians, hospitality ministers, and liturgical environment ministers (formerly decorating committee members).

Outreach Committee

Assists the parish in areas of evangelization through its outreach activities such as home visitation, feeding the homeless and bi monthly Family Fun Nite.

Youth & Young Adult Committee

Responsible for preparing the next generation of leaders for the Catholic Church. The Youth will be engaged in faith and leadership formation, fellowship with Youth around the World, and team building activities.
